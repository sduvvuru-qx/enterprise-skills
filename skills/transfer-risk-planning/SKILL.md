# transfer-risk-planning

Use when quality systems work is about production transfer and risk designation rather than design-quality forms alone.

## When to Use

- JP to US transfer risk
- FormA / transfer-risk discussions
- activity ranking questions
- production-transfer quality planning

## Workflow

1. Identify the transfer scenario:
   - source site
   - target site
   - product/process scope
2. Frame risk categories:
   - product complexity
   - process difficulty
   - supplier readiness
   - workforce / equipment transfer readiness
3. Link transfer-risk framing to downstream quality activity level and schedule intensity.
4. Report what the transfer-risk view implies for project action, not just the risk label.

## Key Decisions

- **Transfer-risk task**: start with FormA / N10 logic.
- **Activity intensity task**: include X10 activity rank and schedule implications.
- **Project-local detail missing**: keep the answer at quality systems-pattern level instead of inventing scores.

## Gotchas

- Treating transfer-risk as interchangeable with DFMEA/DRBFM.
- Giving risk labels without explaining the activity/schedule consequence.
- Missing the JP-to-US transfer lens entirely.
